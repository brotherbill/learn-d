# Factor

A **factor** of a number is an integer that multiplies with another integer to produce that number.

Example:
```
10 = 2 × 5
```

So **2 and 5 are factors of 10**.

Factors always come in **pairs**.

---

## 1. Formal definition

An integer **d** is a factor of an integer **n** if:

```
n = d × k
```

for some integer **k**, and **d ≠ 0**.

Examples:
- 12 = 3 × 4 → 3 and 4 are factors  
- 12 = 2 × 6 → 2 and 6 are factors  
- 12 = 1 × 12 → 1 and 12 are factors  

Negative factors also exist:
- 12 = (−3) × (−4)

But in Learn‑D, we focus on **positive factors** for beginner arithmetic.

---

## 2. Factors vs. divisors

Factors and divisors are the **same idea**, viewed from opposite directions:

- **Factor** → numbers you multiply to get **n**  
- **Divisor** → numbers that divide **n** exactly  

Example:
```
10 = 2 × 5
```

So:
- 2 and 5 are **factors** of 10  
- 2 and 5 are **divisors** of 10  

See **glossary/divisor.md**.

---

## 3. Factor pairs

Factors always come in pairs:

```
n = a × b
```

Examples for 12:
- 1 × 12  
- 2 × 6  
- 3 × 4  

Each pair produces the same number.

---

## 4. Factors and composite numbers

A number is **composite** if it has **more than one factor pair**.

Example:
```
12 = 3 × 4
12 = 2 × 6
12 = 1 × 12
```

Since 12 has multiple factor pairs, it is **composite**.

Prime numbers have exactly **one** factor pair:
```
p = 1 × p
```

See **glossary/composite.md**.

---

## 5. Prime factorization

**Prime factorization** means breaking a number into factors that are **all prime**.

Example:
```
60 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 5
```

Each factor is prime:
- 2  
- 2  
- 3  
- 5  

These primes multiply to give the original number.

Prime factorization is **unique** (for positive integers ≥ 2).  
This is the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic:

> Every positive integer has exactly one prime factorization.

Another example:
```
84 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 7
```

Prime factorization shows the “building blocks” of a number.

---

## 6. Why factors matter

Factors are essential for:

- divisibility  
- prime/composite classification  
- multiplication structure  
- simplifying fractions  
- understanding how numbers break into parts  
- prime factorization  

Factors reveal the internal structure of integers.
