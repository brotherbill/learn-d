# Integer

An **integer** is a whole number.  
Integers include:

- negative whole numbers  
- zero  
- positive whole numbers  

Examples:
```
…, -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, …
```

Integers have **no fractional or decimal part**.

---

## 1. Integers are whole numbers

Integers do not include:

- fractions  
- decimals  
- mixed numbers  

Examples that are **not** integers:
```
1/2
3.14
-7.5
```

---

## 2. Zero is an integer

Zero is part of the integer family:
```
0
```

It is neither positive nor negative.

---

## 3. Positive and negative integers

Positive integers:
```
1, 2, 3, …
```

Negative integers:
```
-1, -2, -3, …
```

Both are integers.

---

## 4. Integers and divisibility

Divisibility is defined whenever multiplication is defined.  
A number **a** is divisible by **b** if:

```
a = b × k
```

for some number **k** in the same number system.

Examples outside the integers:
```
1/2 is divisible by 1/4      (1/2 = (1/4) × 2)
2π is divisible by 2         (2π = 2 × π)
2π is divisible by π         (2π = π × 2)
```

In Learn‑D, we focus on **integer divisibility**, where:

- the numbers are integers  
- the divisor is an integer  
- the quotient must also be an integer  

Example:
```
12 is divisible by 3
15 is divisible by 5
```

Integer divisibility is the foundation for:

- prime numbers  
- composite numbers  
- gcd  
- factorization  
- modular arithmetic

---

## 5. Integers and gcd

The gcd is defined for **pairs of integers**.

Examples:
```
gcd(12, 18) = 6
gcd( 8, 15) = 1
```

Every pair of integers has a gcd ≥ 1.

See **glossary/gcd.md**.

---

## 6. Integers and prime numbers

Prime numbers are **positive integers ≥ 2** with exactly two divisors.

Composite numbers are **positive integers ≥ 4** with more than one factor pair.

See **glossary/prime.md** and **glossary/composite.md**.

---

## 7. Why integers matter

Integers are the foundation of:

- counting  
- divisibility  
- prime structure  
- factorization  
- gcd and lcm  
- modular arithmetic  
- all arithmetic in Learn‑D  

Integers are the basic building blocks of number theory.
