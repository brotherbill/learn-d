# Even

An **even** number is an integer that is divisible by **2** with **no remainder**.

Example:
```
10 ÷ 2 = 5   (no remainder)
```

So **10 is even**.

Example of a non‑even number:
```
9 ÷ 2 = 4 remainder 1
```

So **9 is not even**.

---

## 1. Formal definition

An integer **n** is even if there exists an integer **k** such that:

```
n = 2 × k
```

Examples:
- 0 = 2 × 0  
- 8 = 2 × 4  
- −6 = 2 × (−3)

Evenness applies to **all integers**, positive and negative.

---

## 2. Even numbers are multiples of 2

Every even number is a **multiple of 2**:

```
…, −6, −4, −2, 0, 2, 4, 6, …
```

These are exactly the integers you land on when you count by twos.

---

## 3. Even vs. odd

- **Even** → divisible by 2  
- **Odd** → not divisible by 2  

Odd numbers have remainder 1 when divided by 2.

Example:
```
7 ÷ 2 = 3 remainder 1   → odd
8 ÷ 2 = 4 remainder 0   → even
```

See **glossary/odd.md** (if created).

---

## 4. Why evenness matters

Even numbers are important for:

- divisibility  
- parity checks  
- loop stepping  
- array indexing patterns  
- correctness in algorithms  

Evenness is one of the simplest and most useful arithmetic classifications.
