# Number Theory

**Number theory** is the study of whole numbers and how they relate to each other.  
It focuses on structure, patterns, divisibility, and the building blocks of integers.

Number theory answers questions like:
- What makes a number prime?
- How do numbers factor?
- When does one number divide another?
- What is the greatest common divisor?
- How do numbers behave in patterns?

Number theory is the “geometry” of integers.

---

## 1. The number systems used in number theory

Number theory works mainly with:

### Natural numbers
Whole numbers starting at 0:
```
0, 1, 2, 3, …
```
See **glossary/natural-number.md**.

### Integers
Whole numbers that can be positive, negative, or zero:
```
…, −3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3, …
```
See **glossary/integer.md**.

Number theory does **not** use:
- fractions  
- decimals  
- mixed numbers  

It studies whole-number structure.

---

## 2. Divisibility

Divisibility is the foundation of number theory.

A number **a** is divisible by **b** if:
```
a == b × k
```
for some integer **k**.

Examples:
```
12 is divisible by 3
20 is divisible by 5
```

See **glossary/divisible.md**.

---

## 3. Factors and multiples

A **factor** is a number that divides another number.

A **multiple** is a number you get by multiplying.

Examples:
```
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, …
```

See **glossary/factor.md** and **glossary/multiple.md**.

---

## 4. Prime numbers

A **prime** is a positive integer ≥ 2 with exactly two positive divisors:
- 1  
- itself  

Examples:
```
2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, …
```

Primes are the “atoms” of the positive integers.

See **glossary/prime.md**.

---

## 5. Composite numbers

A **composite** number is a positive integer ≥ 4 that can be factored into smaller positive integers.

Example:
```
10 = 2 × 5
```

Composite numbers are “built” from primes.

See **glossary/composite.md**.

---

## 6. gcd (greatest common divisor)

The **gcd** of two integers is the largest integer that divides both.

Examples:
```
gcd(12, 18) = 6
gcd( 8, 15) = 1
```

The gcd measures how much structure two numbers share.

See **glossary/gcd.md**.

---

## 7. lcm (least common multiple)

The **lcm** of two integers is the smallest positive number that is a multiple of both.

Examples:
```
lcm(4, 6) = 12
lcm(3, 5) = 15
```

The lcm measures how numbers “meet” in their multiples.

See **glossary/lcm.md**.

---

## 8. Parity (even and odd)

Parity describes whether a number is:

- **even** → divisible by 2  
- **odd** → not divisible by 2  

Examples:
```
8 → even
7 → odd
```

Parity is one of the simplest number‑theory classifications.

See **glossary/even.md** and **glossary/odd.md**.

---

## 9. Determinism in number theory

Number theory is fully **deterministic**:

- the same calculation always gives the same result  
- divisibility never changes  
- factors never change  
- primes never change  
- gcd and lcm never change  

Number theory has **no non‑determinism**.  
It is pure structure.

This makes number theory ideal for teaching correctness and reasoning.

See **glossary/deterministic.md**.

---

## 10. Why number theory matters

Number theory teaches:

- structure  
- patterns  
- reasoning  
- correctness  
- divisibility  
- factorization  
- prime behavior  
- modular arithmetic  

It is the foundation of:

- algorithms  
- cryptography  
- computer science  
- mathematics  
- Learn‑D itself  

Number theory is the study of how whole numbers fit together.
