# Natural Number

A **natural number** is a whole number that is **not negative**.  
Natural numbers are used for counting, ordering, and basic arithmetic.

Natural numbers include:

```
0, 1, 2, 3, 4, …
```

Natural numbers have **no fractional or decimal part**.

---

## 1. Natural numbers start at 0

In Learn‑D, natural numbers begin at:

```
0
```

This matches modern mathematics and programming, where 0 is used for:

- counts  
- lengths  
- array indices  
- ranges  
- iteration  

Including 0 keeps the natural numbers complete and structurally correct.

---

## 2. Natural numbers explicitly exclude negative integers

Negative integers are **not** natural numbers.

Examples that are **not** natural numbers:
```
-1
-2
-3
```

Natural numbers are always **non‑negative**.

This boundary is important:

- natural numbers ≥ 0  
- integers can be negative  
- natural numbers do not include negatives  

This keeps the number systems clean and easy to understand.

---

## 3. Natural numbers are whole numbers

Natural numbers do **not** include:

- fractions  
- decimals  
- negative numbers  

Examples that are **not** natural numbers:
```
-3
1/2
3.14
```

Natural numbers are always whole and non‑negative.

---

## 4. Natural numbers and addition

Adding natural numbers always produces another natural number.

Examples:
```
2 + 3 = 5
7 + 0 = 7
```

The result stays inside the natural number family.

---

## 5. Natural numbers and multiplication

Multiplying natural numbers always produces another natural number.

Examples:
```
3 × 4 = 12
5 × 0 = 0
```

Multiplication never leaves the natural numbers.

---

## 6. Natural numbers and divisibility

Divisibility inside the natural numbers means:

```
a == b × k
```

where **a**, **b**, and **k** are natural numbers.

Examples:
```
12 is divisible by 3
20 is divisible by 5
```

Divisibility is the foundation for:

- factors  
- multiples  
- prime numbers  
- composite numbers  

See **glossary/divisible.md**.

---

## 7. Natural numbers and prime numbers

Prime numbers are **natural numbers ≥ 2** with exactly two divisors:

- 1  
- the number itself  

Examples:
```
2, 3, 5, 7, 11, …
```

Composite numbers are natural numbers with more than one factor pair.

See **glossary/prime.md** and **glossary/composite.md**.

---

## 8. Natural numbers and gcd

The gcd (greatest common divisor) is defined for natural numbers and integers.

Examples:
```
gcd(12, 18) = 6
gcd( 8, 15) = 1
```

Natural numbers make gcd easy to understand because they avoid negatives.

See **glossary/gcd.md**.

---

## 9. Why natural numbers matter

Natural numbers are the foundation of:

- counting  
- ordering  
- divisibility  
- prime structure  
- factorization  
- gcd and lcm  
- early arithmetic  
- all number theory in Learn‑D  

Natural numbers are the starting point of structured mathematics.
